Watermelon Mint Juice Recipe

This refreshing and delicious juice is a great way to get a boost of hydration and nutrients on a hot summer day.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound watermelon, peeled, seeded, and cut into chunks
  • 1/2 cup fresh mint leaves, plus more for garnish
  • 1/4 cup lime juice
  • 1 teaspoon sugar (optional)
  • Ice cubes, for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a blender, combine the watermelon, mint leaves, lime juice, and sugar (if using). Blend until smooth.
  2. Strain the juice through a fine-mesh sieve into a pitcher.
  3. Serve over ice, garnished with mint leaves.

Tips:

  • For a more intense mint flavor, add a few more mint leaves to the blender.
  • If you don't have a blender, you can mash the watermelon and mint leaves together with a fork or potato masher. Then, strain the juice through a fine-mesh sieve.
  • You can also add a splash of club soda or ginger ale to the juice for a bit of fizz.
  • This juice is best served immediately, but it can also be stored in the refrigerator for up to 24 hours.

Health Benefits:

  • Watermelon and mint are both very healthy fruits. Watermelon is a good source of vitamins A and C, as well as lycopene, an antioxidant that has been linked to a reduced risk of cancer. Mint is a good source of vitamin C and menthol, which has a cooling effect on the body.
  • This juice is a great way to get a boost of hydration and nutrients on a hot summer day. It's also a refreshing and delicious way to cool down.
