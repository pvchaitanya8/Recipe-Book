Veggie Stir Fry

Veggie Stir Fry is a delicious and nutritious dish that can be prepared quickly and easily. Here's a step-by-step preparation method to make Veggie Stir Fry:

Ingredients:

  • Assorted vegetables (e.g., broccoli, carrots, bell peppers, snap peas, mushrooms, zucchini, onions, etc.), washed and chopped into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil or sesame oil
  • 3-4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of fresh ginger, grated
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce (or tamari for a gluten-free option)
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce (optional, for added umami flavor)
  • 1 tablespoon hoisin sauce (optional, for a touch of sweetness)
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch (or arrowroot powder) mixed with 1/4 cup water (for thickening the sauce)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Cooked rice or noodles for serving

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the vegetables: Wash the vegetables thoroughly and chop them into bite-sized pieces. You can use a variety of colorful vegetables to make the stir-fry visually appealing and packed with nutrients.
  2. Preheat the pan: Place a wok or large skillet over medium-high heat. Once it's hot, add the vegetable oil or sesame oil and swirl it around to coat the bottom of the pan.
  3. Add garlic and ginger: Add the minced garlic and grated ginger to the hot oil. Stir-fry for about 30 seconds until they become fragrant. Be careful not to burn them.
  4. Stir-fry the vegetables: Add the chopped vegetables to the pan. Toss them quickly to coat them evenly with the garlic and ginger. Stir-fry the vegetables for about 3-5 minutes until they are slightly tender but still crisp. You can adjust the cooking time depending on your preference for crunchiness.
  5. Prepare the sauce: In a small bowl, mix the soy sauce, oyster sauce (if using), and hoisin sauce (if using) together. These sauces will provide a savory, salty, and slightly sweet flavor to the stir fry.
  6. Thicken the sauce: Pour the cornstarch (or arrowroot) mixture into the pan with the vegetables. Stir everything together to coat the vegetables evenly. The sauce will thicken and become glossy as it cooks.
  7. Season to taste: Taste the stir-fry and adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper, as needed. Be cautious with the salt if your soy sauce is already quite salty.
  8. Serve: Once the sauce has thickened, and the vegetables are cooked to your liking, turn off the heat. Serve the Veggie Stir Fry over cooked rice or noodles for a complete and satisfying meal.

Optional garnishes: You can garnish the Veggie Stir Fry with sliced green onions, sesame seeds, or chopped cilantro for added freshness and flavor.

Enjoy your delicious and healthy Veggie Stir Fry! Feel free to customize it with your favorite vegetables and adjust the seasonings according to your taste preferences.
