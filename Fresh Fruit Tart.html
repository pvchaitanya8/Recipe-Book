Fresh Fruit Tart

A delicious and visually appealing dessert that combines a buttery tart crust, a creamy pastry cream filling, and an assortment of fresh fruits on top.

Ingredients:

For the Tart Crust:

  • 1 and 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup powdered sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, chilled and cut into small cubes
  • 1 large egg yolk
  • 1-2 tablespoons ice-cold water

For the Pastry Cream:

  • 1 and 1/2 cups whole milk
  • 1/3 cup granulated sugar
  • 3 large egg yolks
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

For the Fresh Fruit Topping:

  • A variety of fresh fruits (strawberries, blueberries, raspberries, kiwi, peaches, etc.)
  • Apricot or peach jam for glazing (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Tart Crust:
    1. In a food processor, combine the flour, powdered sugar, and salt. Pulse a few times to mix.
    2. Add the chilled butter cubes to the processor and pulse until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
    3. Add the egg yolk and 1 tablespoon of ice-cold water. Pulse again until the dough comes together. If needed, add another tablespoon of water slowly until the dough forms a ball.
    4. Remove the dough from the food processor, flatten it into a disk, wrap it in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.
  2. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Grease a tart pan with a removable bottom.
  3. Roll out the chilled tart dough on a floured surface until it's about 1/8 inch thick and larger than the size of your tart pan.
  4. Gently lift the rolled dough and place it into the tart pan. Press the dough into the pan and up the sides, trimming any excess from the edges.
  5. Prick the bottom of the tart crust with a fork to prevent it from puffing up during baking.
  6. Line the crust with parchment paper or aluminum foil and fill it with pie weights or dried beans to keep the crust flat during baking.
  7. Bake the tart crust in the preheated oven for about 15 minutes. Then, remove the parchment paper and weights and bake for an additional 5-8 minutes or until the crust turns golden brown. Allow it to cool completely.
  8. Pastry Cream:
    1. In a saucepan, heat the milk over medium heat until it starts to simmer. Remove it from the heat.
    2. In a separate bowl, whisk together the egg yolks, sugar, and cornstarch until smooth and pale.
    3. Slowly pour the hot milk into the egg mixture, whisking continuously.
    4. Return the mixture to the saucepan and cook over medium heat, stirring constantly until it thickens and comes to a boil.
    5. Remove from heat and stir in the vanilla extract. Transfer the pastry cream to a bowl, cover the surface with plastic wrap to prevent a skin from forming, and let it cool to room temperature.
  9. Assembling the Fresh Fruit Tart:
    1. Once the tart crust and pastry cream have cooled, spread the pastry cream evenly inside the tart crust.
    2. Wash and dry the fresh fruits. Slice larger fruits like strawberries and kiwi, if desired.
    3. Arrange the fresh fruits on top of the pastry cream in a decorative pattern.
    4. If desired, warm some apricot or peach jam and brush it lightly over the fruits to give them a shiny glaze.
  10. Chill the Fresh Fruit Tart in the refrigerator for at least an hour before serving. This will help set the pastry cream and enhance the flavors.
  11. Slice and serve the Fresh Fruit Tart, and enjoy!

Note: You can get creative with the fruit combinations, using seasonal fruits to make the tart even more delightful.
