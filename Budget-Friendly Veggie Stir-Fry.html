Budget-Friendly Veggie Stir-Fry

Stir-Fry with Broccoli, Carrots, and Snow Peas

This stir-fry is a quick and easy way to get a healthy and delicious meal on the table. It's packed with vegetables, and the sauce is both flavorful and easy to make.

Here is the recipe:

  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1/2 onion, chopped
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 1 cup carrots, sliced
  • 1/2 cup snow peas
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons water
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon cornstarch
  • 1/2 teaspoon ginger, grated
  • 1/4 teaspoon garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat.
  2. Add the onion and bell pepper and cook, stirring occasionally, until softened, about 5 minutes.
  3. Add the broccoli, carrots, and snow peas and cook, stirring occasionally, until the vegetables are tender-crisp, about 5 minutes more.
  4. Meanwhile, whisk together the soy sauce, water, brown sugar, cornstarch, ginger, and garlic in a small bowl.
  5. Add the sauce mixture to the skillet or wok and cook, stirring constantly, until the sauce thickens and coats the vegetables, about 1 minute.
  6. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  7. Serve immediately over rice or noodles.

Tips:

  • For a more flavorful stir-fry, use a variety of vegetables. Some other good options include mushrooms, snap peas, bok choy, and zucchini.
  • If you don't have cornstarch, you can substitute another thickener like arrowroot powder or tapioca starch.
  • To make this recipe vegan, use vegetable broth instead of soy sauce.
  • Serve this stir-fry with your favorite sides, like rice, noodles, or quinoa.
  • Enjoy!
