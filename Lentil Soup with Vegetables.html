Lentil Soup with Vegetables

Lentil Soup Recipe

This lentil soup is a hearty and flavorful meal that is perfect for a cold winter day. It is made with simple ingredients that you probably already have on hand, and it is easy to make.

Here is the recipe:

  • Ingredients:
    • 2 tablespoons olive oil
    • 1 onion, chopped
    • 2 carrots, chopped
    • 2 celery stalks, chopped
    • 2 cloves garlic, minced
    • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
    • 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
    • 1/4 teaspoon ground coriander
    • 1/4 teaspoon salt
    • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
    • 2 cups dried lentils, rinsed and picked over
    • 6 cups vegetable broth
    • 1 (14.5 ounce) can diced tomatoes, undrained
    • Chopped fresh parsley, for garnish (optional)
  • Instructions:
    1. Heat the olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the onion, carrots, and celery and cook until softened, about 5 minutes.
    2. Add the garlic, thyme, cumin, coriander, salt, and pepper and cook for 1 minute more.
    3. Stir in the lentils, broth, and tomatoes. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 30-40 minutes, or until the lentils are tender.
    4. Puree half of the soup in a blender until smooth. Stir the pureed soup back into the pot and heat through.
    5. Serve hot, garnished with parsley if desired.
  • Tips:
    • You can use any type of lentils you like in this soup. Green lentils will cook faster than brown lentils, so keep that in mind when you're timing the cooking.
    • If you don't have vegetable broth, you can use chicken broth or water. Just be sure to adjust the seasonings accordingly.
    • This soup is a great way to use up leftover vegetables. If you have any carrots, celery, or onions that are starting to get soft, chop them up and add them to the soup.
    • For a thicker soup, you can mash some of the lentils with a fork before stirring them back into the pot.
    • Serve this soup with crusty bread or crackers for a complete meal.
    • Enjoy!
