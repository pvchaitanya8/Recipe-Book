Chicken Tikka Masala (Indian)

Chicken Tikka Masala is a popular Indian dish known for its tender pieces of marinated chicken served in a rich and flavorful tomato-based sauce. Below is a preparation method to make Chicken Tikka Masala at home:

Ingredients:

For the Chicken Tikka:

  • 1 pound (450g) boneless, skinless chicken breasts or thighs, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1/2 cup plain yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste (a mix of crushed ginger and garlic)
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper (adjust to your spice preference)
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon salt

For the Tikka Masala Sauce:

  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil or ghee (clarified butter)
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 3-4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, grated
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 1 can (14 ounces) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 cup heavy cream or full-fat coconut milk for a dairy-free version
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper (adjust to your spice preference)
  • 1 teaspoon sugar (optional, to balance the acidity of tomatoes)
  • Salt, to taste
  • Fresh cilantro (coriander leaves), chopped, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Marinating the Chicken:
    • In a bowl, combine the yogurt, lemon juice, ginger-garlic paste, ground cumin, ground coriander, ground turmeric, garam masala, cayenne pepper, black pepper, and salt.
    • Add the chicken pieces to the marinade, ensuring they are well-coated. Cover the bowl and let it marinate in the refrigerator for at least 1-2 hours, preferably overnight, to allow the flavors to develop.
  2. Grilling or Broiling the Chicken:
    • Preheat your grill or broiler. If using a grill, preheat it to medium-high heat. If broiling, set your oven to the "broil" setting.
    • Thread the marinated chicken pieces onto skewers. If using wooden skewers, make sure to soak them in water for 30 minutes before using to prevent burning.
    • Grill or broil the chicken skewers for about 10-15 minutes, turning occasionally, until the chicken is cooked through and has a slight char. Cooking times may vary, so keep an eye on them to avoid overcooking.
  3. Preparing the Tikka Masala Sauce:
    • In a large saucepan or skillet, heat the vegetable oil or ghee over medium heat.
    • Add the finely chopped onions and cook until they become soft and translucent.
    • Stir in the minced garlic and grated ginger, cooking for another minute until fragrant.
    • Add the tomato paste, ground cumin, ground coriander, garam masala, paprika, and cayenne pepper. Cook the spices with the onion mixture for a couple of minutes to release their flavors.
    • Pour in the crushed tomatoes and let the sauce simmer for about 10 minutes, allowing it to thicken and develop its flavors.
    • Stir in the heavy cream or coconut milk and continue to simmer for another 5 minutes. Add sugar if desired, and season with salt according to taste.
  4. Combining the Chicken and Sauce:
    • Once the chicken is done grilling or broiling, remove the pieces from the skewers and add them to the Tikka Masala sauce.
    • Let the chicken simmer in the sauce for a few minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.
  5. Serving:
    • Garnish the Chicken Tikka Masala with chopped cilantro (coriander leaves).
    • Serve the dish hot, accompanied by steamed basmati rice or Indian bread like naan or roti.

Enjoy your homemade Chicken Tikka Masala, a delightful Indian dish bursting with rich flavors!
